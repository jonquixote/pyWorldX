**Question:** We know EROI changes with Solar/Wind, but do biophysical models explicitly track 'Rare Earth Element' (REE) constraints? If Silver, Neodymium, or Indium hit their mass-balance limits, exactly how does the model 'choke' the deployment rate of new Green Energy Capital?

---

Answer:
Yes, modern biophysical models like WORLD6 and WORLD7 explicitly track Rare 
Earth Elements (REEs) and specific technology metals because they are recognized
as absolute prerequisites for transitioning to a sustainable energy paradigm [1,
2]. 

Rather than lumping resources together, these models feature highly 
disaggregated modules that track individual mass-balances for precious metals 
(like Silver), technology metals (like Indium, Gallium, and Tellurium), and Rare
Earth Elements (including Neodymium, tracked under the REE module) [3-6].

Here is exactly how these models mechanically "choke" the deployment of Green 
Energy Capital when mass-balance limits for these elements are hit:

**1. Explicit Demand Linkages**
In the models' architecture, the deployment of alternative energy technologies 
generates a strict material prerequisite. If the system attempts to build solar 
photovoltaics or electric vehicles, the energy module broadcasts a specific 
demand for materials like silver, indium, or rare earths [7, 8]. This required 
mass is mathematically "taken away from their extraction module and moved to the
energy module" [8].

**2. The Transition to "Hard Scarcity"**
The models track physical supply against "price-modified demand" [9]. Because 
these metals are often found in low concentrations, extracting them requires 
climbing a steep entropy hill. As high-grade ores are depleted, the models 
eventually hit a crossover point called **"hard scarcity."** At this stage, 
physical supply fundamentally separates from demand, meaning the mining sector 
cannot output enough material to satisfy the energy sector, regardless of how 
high the market price goes or how much society is willing to pay [9]. 

**3. The Mechanical "Choke" (Production Reduction)**
The throttling of Green Energy Capital is handled via a strict mass-balance 
dependency rule. The models enforce the logic that **"when actual supply to a 
module is less than demand, the production is reduced"** [8, 10]. If the 
required tonnage of Indium or Silver is unavailable, the RK4 arrays 
mathematically prohibit the energy sector from instantiating the requested solar
or wind capital. The deployment rate of the specific technology is 
proportionally capped by the limiting element [7, 8]. 

**4. The 65% Energy Ceiling Feedback**
This choking mechanism is further compounded by thermodynamic limits. Extracting
increasingly dilute, low-grade technology metals requires massive amounts of 
energy [8]. If the energy demanded to mine these elements pushes the total 
global resource extraction sector above **65% of all available energy**, the 
models programmatically reduce the extraction rate of the metals themselves [8, 
10]. This creates a vicious cycle where energy scarcity prevents the mining of 
the very metals needed to build new energy-generating capital.

**The Systemic Implication**
Because of these hard constraints, WORLD7 simulations explicitly demonstrate 
that no single alternative energy technology (whether based on indium, gallium, 
germanium, or telluride) "will not have the capacity alone" to replace fossil 
fuels [9]. Similarly, the models predict that elements like Lithium will enter 
hard scarcity by 2100, physically proving that "there will not be enough lithium
to substitute electric vehicles for all fossil fuel vehicles presently in use" 
[9].
