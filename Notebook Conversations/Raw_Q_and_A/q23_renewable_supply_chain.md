### 1. Modeling the Implementation Delays
While granular supply chain inventory buffers are not explicitly defined in the 
provided text, the models impose massive macroscopic temporal delays to account 
for the deployment of complex technologies:
*   **The 20-Year Technology Delay:** In "Comprehensive Technology" scenarios, 
the massive capital investments required for transitioning to new technologies 
and implementing determined societal decisions face a strict **20-year 
implementation delay** before they become effective [4, 5].
*   **The 50-Year Maintenance Delay:** Models tracking infrastructural 
lifecycles (like WORLD5) incorporate a **50-year delay** between the initial 
heavy capital investment in new energy infrastructures and the time when the 
"Incurred Cost of Maintenance" for infrastructural renewal (which compounds at 
1.5% per year) catches up to drain the system's wealth [6].

### 2. The Mathematical Mechanism of the 65% Energy Ceiling Bottleneck
The bottlenecking of prerequisite transition metals (like Copper, Lithium, or 
Rare Earths) is mathematically modeled through explicit **Demand and Supply 
Linkages** tied to rigid thermodynamic limits:
*   **Explicit Demand Linkages:** When the model attempts to deploy alternative 
energy technologies (like solar panels or wind turbines), the energy module 
generates a strict material demand for specific "Technology Metals" (e.g., 
silver, gallium, indium, lithium, rare earths). This required mass is 
mathematically "taken away from their extraction module and moved to the energy 
module" [7, 8].
*   **Hitting the 65% Energy Ceiling:** Extracting these increasingly dilute 
metals requires immense amounts of energy to "climb the entropy hill." The 
models enforce a hard thermodynamic limit: if the energy demanded by the global 
resource extraction sector exceeds **65% of the total available global energy**,
the extraction rate is programmatically decreased [8-11]. 
*   **The CentralRegistrar's Supply Multipliers:** In the PyWorldX architecture,
this is resolved during the RK4 sub-steps by the `CentralRegistrar`. If the 
aggregate energy demand from extraction hits the 65% ceiling, the registrar 
calculates and broadcasts a **Global Supply Multiplier (<1.0)** to 
proportionally scale back the "Total Tradable Supply" of resources [12].
*   **The Mechanical Choke on Green Capital:** The deployment speed of Green 
Technology Capital is directly choked by a mass-balance dependency rule: "When 
actual supply to a module is less than demand, then the production is reduced" 
[13, 14]. If the 65% energy ceiling prevents the extraction module from mining 
enough Lithium or Copper, the arrays mathematically prohibit the energy sector 
from instantiating the requested renewable capital, capping the green energy 
transition by the availability of the limiting element [7, 13, 14].
