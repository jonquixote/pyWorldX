# Synthesized v2 Scenario Testing Suite

Following the completion of the full 57-query NotebookLM research corpus, this document synthesizes the specific scenario designs required to validate the PyWorldX v2 architecture. Unlike the original World3-03 scenarios (which simply doubled resource stocks or toggled technology switches), these scenarios stress-test **thermodynamic ceilings, financial feedback loops, and regional mass-balances** integrated into the new engine.

## I. The Carrington Event (Fast Process Shock / Black Swan)

This scenario tests the financial layer's vulnerability to sudden physical destruction and proves that the system cannot simply "re-industrialize" without liquidity.

*   **Trigger:** An instantaneous **50% destruction of Industrial Capital** ($IC$) in a single RK4 step — modeling a Carrington-class geomagnetic storm destroying the global electrical grid.
*   **Mechanism:** Physical capital is monetized as collateral ($V_c = \text{Stock} \times \text{Price}$). Destroying 50% of $IC$ instantly vaporizes 50% of the system's collateral value. Meanwhile, amortized legacy debts remain intact. This automatically forces the **Debt-to-GDP ratio past the 1.5 (150%) ceiling**.
*   **Expected Behavior:** The RK4 solver must programmatically force `Investment Rate = 0` due to lack of loan availability. The Maintenance Gap immediately triggers the bounded $\phi(\text{MaintenanceRatio})$ exponential multiplier (2.0–4.0×), accelerating depreciation of all remaining capital. The system enters a **permanent financial liquidity trap** that mathematically prohibits re-industrialization.
*   **What This Proves:** That physical capital destruction creates irreversible financial consequences — the financial layer freezes *before* the physical layer can respond. Re-industrialization is prohibited not by physics, but by accounting.

## II. The Minsky Moment (Financial & Ecological Overshoot)

The Minsky Moment tests the crossover point where the physical cost of maintaining complexity exceeds the wealth generated by the biophysical base.

### IIa. Financial Minsky Moment

*   **Trigger:** The moment when **Total Debt exceeds the Security Value** of all collateralized assets (Industrial, Service, and Agricultural Capital).
*   **Mechanism:** Evaluated via `Financial Resilience = Σ V_c,i − Debt`. When Financial Resilience becomes negative, the engine triggers a multiplier that drops `Investment Rate = 0`, initiating a broad-front systemic collapse.
*   **What This Proves:** That debt is not abstract — it is physically collateralized against depreciating real assets, and when those assets lose value faster than debt is repaid, the system locks up.

### IIb. Nature's Minsky Moment (ESP Tipping)

*   **Trigger:** The **Ecosystem Services Proxy (ESP)** drops below its logistic regeneration threshold, where $\text{DegradationRate} > r(T) \times ESP \times (1 - ESP)$.
*   **Mechanism:** The model forces Industrial Capital to fund Artificial Ecosystem Services (AES) at exponentially rising cost ($c_{AES}$). This drives the Benefit Return on Investment (BeROI) negative and starves the industrial sector of reinvestment capital.
*   **What This Proves:** That ecosystem collapse creates a financial drain identical in structure to a debt crisis — nature's services have implicit market value, and when they vanish, the replacement cost destroys the economy from within.

## III. The "Absolute Decoupling" Null Hypothesis

This scenario is not a forecast — it is a **mathematical proof by contradiction**. It demonstrates that the model enforces thermodynamic laws by systematically disabling them to simulate infinite growth.

*   **Override 1:** Set the physical resource elasticity exponent ($\beta$) to **0** in the Cobb-Douglas production function — making output independent of physical resources.
*   **Override 2:** Clamp FCAOR to a static **0.05** (5%) — preventing extraction costs from rising with ore grade decline.
*   **Override 3:** Disable the **65% Thermodynamic Energy Ceiling** — allowing infinite energy extraction without market throttling.
*   **Override 4:** Set TNDS for R&D to **0** — allowing infinite innovation without capital drain.
*   **Override 5:** Force **100% Phosphorus Recycling Rate** at zero energy cost — bypassing the 85% biophysical stability floor.

*   **Expected Behavior:** With all five overrides active, the model outputs infinitely growing GDP and a stabilized population without triggering overshoot-and-collapse cascades.
*   **What This Proves:** That absolute decoupling of economic growth from physical resource throughput is **only possible if one explicitly programs the engine to violate the laws of mass-balance and thermodynamics**. The null hypothesis is rejected by the un-overridden model.

## IV. Rapid AI Growth vs. AI Stagnation (Computing within Limits)

This scenario family tests the macroeconomic and pollution impacts of hyperscale data center deployment.

*   **Parameters:**
    *   `frac_io_ai_2050 = 0.06` (6% of industrial output devoted to AI)
    *   `ai_CO2_intensity_2020 = 0.15`
    *   `ai_ewaste_intensity_2020 = 3.5e-4`
*   **Mechanism:** AI hardware scaling acts as an entropy trap. Rapid AI scaling generates a **4% higher and later peak** in the persistent pollution stock, deepening downstream feedback loops that trigger agricultural degradation and systemic collapse.
*   **What This Proves:** That computational infrastructure is not "dematerialized" — it has thermodynamic costs that feed back into the pollution and energy ceilings.

## V. The "Giant Leap" / Global Energiewende

This scenario replaces the original "Comprehensive Technology" run by testing a managed, deliberate contraction of fossil fuels.

*   **Trigger:** A programmatic step-down phasing out fossil hydrocarbon extraction by **90% between 2020 and 2060** to cap climate change at a 2°C limit.
*   **Mechanism:** Tests the "Implementation Delay" (20-year technology deployment lag) and the "Material Drag" of the green transition. The massive upfront capital required for renewables drops the Capital Output Ratio (COR) and elevates **Non-Discretionary Investment from 24% to 36% of GDP**. The CentralRegistrar must resolve whether the remaining 35% energy budget (after the 65% ceiling) can sustain the transition capital requirements.
*   **What This Proves:** Whether a society can survive the transition period without hitting the 150% Debt-to-GDP ceiling — testing if the financial layer can bridge a 40-year structural transformation.

## VI. Contagious Disintegration ("Lifeboating")

For regionalized array architectures, this scenario tests network fragmentation.

*   **Triggers:** A region's **Food Per Capita (FPC)** dropping below the subsistence threshold of **230 kg/year**, OR a region breaching the **150% Debt-to-GDP** limit.
*   **Mechanism:** Hitting either trigger forces the "Scale of Centralization" multiplier ($C_{scale}$) to drop from **1.0 to 0.0**. The local node mathematically severs its export linkages to the global Trade Registrar, becoming economically isolated.
*   **Cascade:** The sudden disappearance of exports from the collapsed node starves importing "core" regions of critical resources. Their own FPC and Debt metrics deteriorate, potentially triggering their own $C_{scale}$ collapse — creating a **domino cascade of regional disintegration**.
*   **What This Proves:** That global trade networks are fragile to localized failures, and that wealthy "core" regions cannot maintain their basins of attraction when peripheral suppliers collapse.
