**Question:** The standard scenarios (like high_resource_discovery) test World3-03. What specific scenarios and corresponding parameters should a v2 suite implement to test the new architecture? For example: What specific triggers test the Minsky Moment? What % of Industrial Capital destruction correctly models the Carrington Event scenario?

---

Answer:
To rigorously test the new biophysically-grounded, multi-sector architecture of 
a v2 suite (like PyWorldX, WORLD7, or Earth4All frameworks), the testing 
scenarios must move beyond the original World3's simple parameter tweaks (like 
doubling resources). Instead, they must stress-test the thermodynamic ceilings, 
financial feedback loops, and regional mass-balances integrated into the new 
engine. 

Here are the specific scenarios and corresponding parameters that a v2 suite 
should implement:

### 1. The Carrington Event (Fast Process Shock / Black Swan)
This scenario tests the financial layer's vulnerability to sudden physical 
destruction and proves that the system cannot simply "re-industrialize" without 
liquidity.
*   **Trigger / Parameter:** An instantaneous **50% destruction of Industrial 
Capital** (the Global Grid) in a single RK4 step.
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** Because physical capital is monetized as collateral 
($V_c = Stock \times Price$), destroying 50% of it instantly vaporizes 50% of 
the system's collateral value [1]. Meanwhile, amortized legacy debts remain 
intact. This automatically forces the **Debt-to-GDP ratio to skyrocket past the 
1.5 (150%) ceiling** [2].
*   **Expected Behavior:** The RK4 solver must programmatically force the 
Investment Rate to zero due to lack of loan availability, proving that physical 
destruction creates a permanent financial liquidity trap that prohibits 
recovery.

### 2. The Minsky Moment (Financial & Ecological Overshoot)
The "Minsky Moment" tests the crossover point where the physical cost of 
maintaining complexity exceeds the wealth generated by the biophysical base.
*   **Trigger / Parameter:** The moment when **Total Debt exceeds the Security 
Value** of all collateralized assets (Industrial, Service, and Agricultural 
Capital). 
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** This is evaluated via the equation `Financial 
Resilience = \sum V_{c,i} - Debt` [3]. When Financial Resilience becomes 
negative, the engine must trigger a multiplier that drops the Investment Rate to
zero, initiating a broad-front systemic collapse [3]. 
*   **The "Nature" Variant:** A secondary Minsky Moment is triggered when the 
**Ecosystem Services Proxy (ESP)** drops near zero. The model must force 
Industrial Capital to fund Artificial Ecosystem Services (AES), driving the 
Benefit Return on Investment (BeROI) negative and starving the industrial sector
[4].

### 3. The "Absolute Decoupling" Null Hypothesis
This scenario tests whether the model mathematically enforces the laws of 
thermodynamics by systematically disabling them to simulate infinite growth.
*   **Triggers / Parameters:** 
    1. Set the physical resource elasticity exponent ($\beta$) to **0** in the 
Cobb-Douglas production function.
    2. Clamp the Fraction of Capital Allocated to Obtaining Resources (FCAOR) to
a static **0.05** (5%), ignoring ore grade decline.
    3. Disable the **65% Thermodynamic Energy Ceiling**.
    4. Set the Total Non-Discretionary Spending (TNDS) for R&D to **0**.
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** By overriding these mass-balance integrators, the 
engine should output infinitely growing GDP, mathematically proving that 
absolute decoupling requires violating the laws of physics.

### 4. Rapid AI Growth vs. AI Stagnation (Computing within Limits)
Drawing from recent limits-to-computing recalibrations, this scenario family 
tests the macroeconomic and pollution impacts of hyperscale data centers.
*   **Triggers / Parameters:** The deployment of AI is introduced into the 
Persistent Pollution and Capital sectors using parameters like `frac_io_ai_2050`
(the target share of industrial output devoted to AI, e.g., set to **6.0e-2** or
6%), `ai_CO2_intensity_2020` (**1.5e-1**), and `ai_ewaste_intensity_2020` 
(**3.5e-4**) [5-8]. 
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** Tests whether the exponential scaling of AI hardware 
acts as an entropy trap. Rapid AI scaling should generate a 4% higher and later 
peak in the persistent pollution stock, deepening the downstream feedback loops 
that trigger agricultural degradation and systemic collapse [9].

### 5. The "Giant Leap" / Global Energiewende
This scenario replaces the original "Comprehensive Technology" run by testing a 
managed, deliberate contraction of fossil fuels combined with Earth4All's 
"extraordinary turnarounds" [10].
*   **Triggers / Parameters:** A programmatic step-down phasing out fossil 
hydrocarbon extraction by **90% between 2020 and 2060** to cap climate change at
a $2^\circ$C limit [11].
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** Tests the "Implementation Delay" and the "Material 
Drag" of the green transition. The massive upfront capital required for 
renewables must drop the Capital Output Ratio (COR) and elevate 
Non-Discretionary Investment (e.g., from 24% up to 36% of GDP) [12]. The engine 
will test if the society can survive the transition without hitting the 150% 
Debt-to-GDP ceiling.

### 6. Contagious Disintegration ("Lifeboating")
If the v2 suite utilizes a regionalized array architecture, this scenario tests 
network fragmentation.
*   **Triggers / Parameters:** A region's **Food Per Capita (FPC)** dropping 
below the subsistence threshold of **230 kg/year**, or a region breaching the 
**150% Debt-to-GDP** limit [13].
*   **Mechanisms Tested:** Hitting these parameters forces the "Scale of 
Centralization" multiplier ($C_{scale}$) to drop from **1.0 to 0.0**. The local 
node mathematically severs its export linkages to the global Trade Registrar 
[14]. This tests the "Contagion of Collapse," measuring how rapidly the sudden 
lack of imports knocks wealthy "core" regions out of their basins of attraction 
and into a cascading financial collapse [15].
