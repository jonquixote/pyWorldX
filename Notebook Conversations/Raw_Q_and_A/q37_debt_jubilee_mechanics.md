**Question:** When the 150% Debt-to-GDP limit is hit, do models like WORLD7 allow for a programmatic 'Debt Jubilee' (canceling debts)? If so, does explicitly resetting the debt array actually prevent a biophysical collapse, or does it merely vaporize the 'Liquid Funds' of the Capital owners, precipitating the crash anyway?

---

Answer:
In the **WORLD7** model architecture, the **150% Debt-to-GDP limit** functions 
as a hard programmatic constraint that prevents a region or the global system 
from taking on new loans to replenish its **Liquid Funds** once this threshold 
is crossed [1]. While the model is highly suited for testing unconventional 
policy interventions, the literature indicates that a "Debt Jubilee" or explicit
reset of the debt array provides only a temporary financial reprieve that 
cannot, by itself, prevent a **biophysical collapse**.

### 1. The Function of the 150% Debt Limit
In **WORLD7**, debts are incurred to fund investments and maintain the stock of 
**Liquid Cash** (the "cash to pay bills") [1]. 
*   **The Borrowing Cap:** Once the debt-to-GDP ratio exceeds 150%, the model 
programmatically blocks the "Take up loan" flow [1, 2]. 
*   **The Financial Squeeze:** At this point, the system can no longer "borrow 
from the future" to bridge fluctuations or fund the escalating costs of resource
extraction [3, 4]. This forces the system to rely solely on existing **Liquid 
Funds**, which are rapidly depleted by interest payments (typically 3%) and the 
**Incurred Cost of Maintenance** for complex infrastructures [1, 5].

### 2. Debt Jubilee: Bridging vs. Resolving Structural Problems
While the model can be used to simulate a programmatic resetting of the debt 
array (a Jubilee), the sources are explicit that financial manipulation does not
resolve underlying physical realities.
*   **Fluctuation vs. Structure:** Debt is described as an effective way to 
"bridge fluctuations in the economy," but the authors warn it **"will not 
resolve structural problems"** unless the credit is used to implement 
fundamental structural change (e.g., a sustainability transition) [3, 6].
*   **Contraction is Unavoidable:** If biophysical expenditures (the energy and 
capital required to maintain a complex society) continue to grow while 
resource-driven income stagnates, a **contraction becomes unavoidable** 
regardless of the debt level [5, 7].

### 3. Does Resetting Debt Prevent Collapse?
The literature suggests that resetting the debt array is unlikely to prevent 
collapse because the primary drivers of overshoot are biophysical, not 
financial.
*   **The 'Entropy Hill' Problem:** The system's decline is driven by the need 
to climb the **"entropy hill"**—investing more energy and capital to extract 
increasingly low-grade, high-entropy resources [8, 9]. Canceling debts clears 
the financial ledger but does not replenish the physical resource base or 
restore a high **EROI** [7, 10].
*   **The Maintenance Gap:** A core mechanism of collapse in these models is the
**Tainter-style "cost over wealth" overshoot** [5]. Even with zero debt, the 
physical **Incurred Cost of Maintenance** for infrastructure, aging populations 
(pensions), and climate damages eventually exceeds the wealth generated by the 
biophysical base [5, 11, 12].

### 4. Vaporization of Liquid Funds and Financial Resilience
Explicitly resetting the debt array in a model like WORLD7 often precipitates 
the crash it was meant to avoid by **vaporizing the financial resilience** of 
capital owners.
*   **Debt as an Asset:** In the model's "Double Accounting" approach, debt is a
claim on future wealth and an asset for the **Capital Owners** and **Pension 
Funds** [4, 13, 14]. 
*   **Loss of Resilience:** The sources state that when loans exceed assets or 
are wiped out, the system loses its **financial resilience** [11]. This 
vaporization of "Liquid Funds" means that even if a society wants to implement a
technological fix, **"no more money can be raised for the necessary change"** 
[11].
*   **National Suicide:** The authors characterize economic growth based solely 
on debt growth as **"national suicide in the long run,"** implying that once the
biophysical limit is hit, financial "tricks" merely accelerate the transition 
from a managed decline to a chaotic collapse [15].

In summary, while **WORLD7** allows for the testing of debt cancellation, the 
model's "Causality-Based" logic dictates that you cannot "print" or "cancel" 
your way out of a biophysical deficit. Resetting the debt array may clear a 
spreadsheet, but it does nothing to lower the **Thermodynamic Energy Ceiling** 
or close the **Maintenance Gap** that ultimately breaks the industrial base.
